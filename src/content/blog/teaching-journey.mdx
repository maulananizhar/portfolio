## My Teaching Practice Journey at SMKN 40 Jakarta

One of the most memorable experiences during my university journey was participating in Praktik Keterampilan Mengajar (PKM) at SMKN 40 Jakarta.

As a student of Pendidikan Teknik Informatika dan Komputer at Universitas Negeri Jakarta, this program became my first real experience teaching students directly in a classroom environment. More than just completing academic requirements, PKM gave me a new perspective on what it truly means to become an educator.

During the program, I learned not only how to deliver materials, but also how to communicate, manage classrooms, adapt to different student personalities, and build confidence in front of students.

## First Impressions of SMKN 40 Jakarta

At the beginning of the program, I started by observing the school environment and learning about the overall teaching atmosphere at SMKN 40 Jakarta.

The school is located in East Jakarta, near Universitas Islam Jakarta and several public facilities. From the first day, I noticed that the school environment felt active and welcoming. The relationships between teachers, staff, and students were also very positive, creating a comfortable learning atmosphere.

During the observation period, I also learned about the school facilities, classroom layouts, laboratory usage, and daily teaching schedules.

This observation phase became very important because it helped me understand how the school operated before I started teaching directly.

## Preparing to Become a Teacher

Before entering the classroom, I spent time preparing teaching materials, lesson plans, and learning media that would later be used during the teaching sessions.

This process made me realize that teaching is not only about speaking in front of students. A teacher must also think about:

- How students understand materials
- How to make learning engaging
- How to explain concepts clearly
- How to manage time effectively
- How to maintain classroom focus

I prepared PowerPoint presentations, learning modules, and classroom activities to help students follow the lessons more comfortably.

At this stage, I also started learning how to balance educational content with interactive learning approaches.

## My First Teaching Experience

One of the most unforgettable moments during PKM was entering the classroom for the first time as a teacher.

At the beginning of the session, I greeted the students, introduced myself, and invited them to introduce themselves one by one. Although I was nervous at first, the interaction gradually became more natural as the class continued.

During the first meeting, I explained the overview of the materials that would be studied throughout the semester and discussed the learning agreements that would be applied during class activities.

I realized that students become more engaged when the teacher creates a comfortable and approachable atmosphere.

That first classroom experience became a turning point for me because it helped build my confidence as a future educator.

## Learning How to Manage a Classroom

As the teaching sessions continued, I started learning more about classroom management.

Every class had different characteristics. Some students were very active and responsive, while others were quieter and needed more encouragement to participate.

This taught me that teaching cannot use only one approach for every student.

I learned how to:

- Maintain classroom attention
- Encourage student participation
- Adapt explanations based on student understanding
- Build communication with students
- Handle classroom situations calmly

Over time, I became more comfortable interacting with students and speaking in front of the class.

## Daily Activities During PKM

My activities during PKM were not limited to teaching alone.

Throughout the program, I was also involved in:

- School observations
- Preparing teaching materials
- Participating in school activities
- Supervising classes
- Completing teaching documentation
- Carrying out scheduled duties and responsibilities

The teaching schedule itself was quite intensive, starting in the morning until the afternoon on several days each week.

Experiencing the daily routines of teachers gave me a much deeper appreciation for the responsibilities and dedication required in the education field.

## Challenges During the Experience

Like any real-world experience, PKM also came with challenges.

Some of the challenges I observed included limited classroom facilities, laboratory availability, and classroom conditions that were sometimes less comfortable due to heat and infrastructure limitations.

However, these situations also taught me adaptability.

I learned that teachers often need to remain professional and continue creating effective learning environments despite limitations.

This experience helped me understand that education is not always about having perfect facilities, but also about creativity, patience, and commitment in the learning process.

## Building Connections with Students and Teachers

One thing I appreciated the most during PKM was the opportunity to interact closely with both students and teachers.

The teachers at SMKN 40 Jakarta were supportive and willing to guide us throughout the program. I often received feedback, suggestions, and encouragement regarding teaching approaches and classroom interaction.

Meanwhile, interactions with students became one of the most enjoyable parts of the experience.

Seeing students actively ask questions, respond to explanations, and participate during class activities gave me a sense of fulfillment that was difficult to describe.

Those moments made me realize how meaningful the role of a teacher can be.

## What I Learned from PKM

PKM taught me many things beyond teaching theory.

I learned:

- How to communicate more effectively
- How to become more confident in public speaking
- How to manage responsibilities professionally
- How to understand student behavior
- How to adapt in real classroom situations
- How important patience and empathy are in education

Most importantly, the experience helped me grow both personally and professionally.

I became more disciplined, more confident, and more aware of the responsibilities carried by educators.

## Closing Thoughts

Looking back, my teaching practice journey at SMKN 40 Jakarta became one of the most valuable experiences during my university life.

It was not only about fulfilling academic requirements, but also about understanding the realities of education and experiencing firsthand what it means to guide students in a classroom environment.

Through this experience, I gained memories, lessons, and perspectives that will continue to influence my journey moving forward — both as a technology enthusiast and as someone who values education deeply.
