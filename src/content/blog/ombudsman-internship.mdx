## My Internship Journey at Ombudsman Republik Indonesia

During my internship at Ombudsman Republik Indonesia, I had the opportunity to work under the Bureau of Public Relations and Information Technology (HMTI) as a Fullstack Developer. Over the course of two months, I experienced what it felt like to contribute directly inside a government institution while collaborating with internal IT teams and fellow interns.

This internship was not only about building systems, but also about understanding how technology supports public services and how digital transformation is implemented within a large institution.

### Starting the Internship Journey

My internship officially began in March 2025. During the first few days, I was introduced to the working environment, internal regulations, and the systems used by Ombudsman Republik Indonesia.

One of the first activities I participated in was presenting the technologies and programming languages I had learned during university. I also shared several web projects I had previously developed and explained the libraries and tools I commonly used.

This initial stage helped the supervisors understand our technical background and determine how we could contribute during the internship period.

### Learning About Business Process Reengineering

Before entering the development phase, I was introduced to the concept of Business Process Reengineering (BPR).

At this stage, I learned how organizations evaluate and redesign workflows to improve efficiency and service quality. This became an important foundation because the projects we worked on were closely related to optimizing digital services within Ombudsman RI.

Together with the team, I attended discussions and presentations regarding the internal workflow of several systems used inside the institution.

### Analyzing SIMPeL4

One of the systems I analyzed was SIMPeL4, a platform used to process and manage public complaints.

Through direct presentations from the internal team, I learned how reports from the public are received, verified, and processed by Ombudsman RI.

During this phase, I focused on observing the user experience and overall workflow from the perspective of public users.

I realized that designing systems for public services requires more than just functionality. Accessibility, clarity, and ease of use are equally important because the users come from various backgrounds.

This experience taught me how important user-centered thinking is when building digital systems intended for the public.

### Getting Introduced to the Helpdesk Project

After completing the analysis phase, I was introduced to a new internal project: the Ombudsman Helpdesk System.

The project aimed to create an internal helpdesk platform that could help employees submit requests, communicate with administrators, and track ongoing issues more efficiently.

At the beginning, I spent time understanding the structure of the existing system and learning how the frontend, backend, and admin platforms were connected.

This process exposed me to real-world collaborative development environments where multiple repositories and systems work together simultaneously.

### Discovering Problems and Evaluating the System

One of the most interesting parts of the internship was evaluating the existing system.

While exploring the platform, I identified several issues related to user experience, workflow efficiency, and system usability.

Some of the things I observed included:

- Interfaces that felt inconsistent
- Dashboard flows that required too many steps
- Features that were difficult to access quickly
- Design components that did not match the overall interface
- User interactions that could be made more intuitive

Rather than focusing only on the technical side, I learned how important it is to analyze systems from the perspective of actual users.

I also participated in discussions with supervisors regarding possible improvements and how the system could better support internal workflows.

### Designing the New Helpdesk Experience

After presenting the evaluation results, our team was given the opportunity to continue developing the Helpdesk system.

At this stage, I focused heavily on UI/UX design and user experience improvements.

I worked on designing several pages such as:

- Login page
- Register page
- User dashboard
- Request tracking page
- Messaging section
- Testimonial section

One thing I enjoyed the most was learning how to create interfaces that were not only visually clean, but also easy to understand for users.

I spent a lot of time exploring design references, thinking about navigation flow, and discussing interface ideas with teammates.

This process taught me that good design is not just about aesthetics, but also about reducing confusion and helping users complete their tasks efficiently.

### Collaborating with the Team

Throughout the internship, collaboration became one of the most valuable experiences.

I worked closely with fellow interns and internal staff during discussions, presentations, evaluations, and development sessions.

We regularly presented progress updates, discussed ideas, received feedback, and revised our work based on suggestions from supervisors.

Working inside a professional environment taught me how important communication and teamwork are in software development.

I learned how to:

- Present ideas professionally
- Explain technical concepts clearly
- Accept feedback constructively
- Coordinate tasks within a team
- Adapt to existing workflows

### Presenting Progress to the Institution

One of the memorable moments during the internship was presenting our progress directly to the HMTI bureau.

From the initial analysis phase until the final project presentation, we continuously shared the development progress and received input from supervisors.

At the end of the internship period, we presented the final result of the Helpdesk project.

Seeing the system evolve from early analysis and discussions into a functioning platform became one of the most rewarding parts of the experience.

### What I Learned from the Internship

My internship at Ombudsman Republik Indonesia gave me a much broader understanding of software development beyond the classroom.

I learned that building systems inside real institutions involves:

- Understanding organizational workflows
- Communicating with stakeholders
- Designing for real users
- Working collaboratively
- Adapting to constraints and feedback
- Thinking critically about usability and efficiency

More importantly, I realized how technology can directly support public services and improve operational processes inside institutions.

### Closing Thoughts

Looking back, this internship became one of the most meaningful experiences in my journey as a developer.

It was my first experience contributing to projects inside a government institution, collaborating in a professional environment, and participating in the full process of system analysis, design, and development.

Beyond the technical experience, the internship helped me grow personally and professionally.

It taught me how to communicate better, work in teams, adapt quickly, and approach problems from both technical and user perspectives.

Most importantly, it strengthened my motivation to continue learning and building technology that can create meaningful impact for people and organizations.
